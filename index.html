The Methodological Problem

Cognitive phenomena are not directly perceptible. How, then, can we analyze the internal processes of persuasion?

The proposed solution is the analysis of endoxa: the volatile, imprecise, and diffuse truths a community holds.

Functional Endoxa

These reveal how persuasion is conceived, offering vestiges of the interaction between the two cognitive systems.

Substantive Endoxa

These are the specific arguments an audience accepts, revealing the reaction to that cognitive interaction.

The Rationale for Comparison

Why seek functional endoxa in Aristotle's Rhetoric and the Zhànguó Cè?

Both texts were conceived for circumstances lacking clear guidance for decision-making, in the absence of:

A logos sustained by strong, unwavering axioms.

(Greece)

A rigid ritual order like that of the 周 (Zhou) dynasty.

(China)

Context

The decline of the Ritual system (禮) necessitated a new praxis. Language is consequently employed as an instrument to secure practical advantage (實).
